Visual Style

Makoto Shinkai’s style can be described as the heightened version of reality, where colors are often saturated and objects are drawn shinier than its real counterpart.


Makoto Shinkai’s lighting involves creating great contrast between areas that are in shadow and areas with light. This ligthing allows him to make textures standout even further.


秒速5センチメートル[2007]

5 Centimeters per Second

言の葉の庭 [2013]

Garden of Words

Often his works use prominent blue-hues and thin shadows often have outlines.


Lens flares are often used, likely to imitate what a camera sees.

Characters are seperated from the background by having them cel-shaded which makes them sharper. This makes the characters/people stand out from the background.


As Makoto Shinkai grew as a director, he adopts are more Ghibli-style to his backgrounds. His backgrounds become softer and implement more of a painting aesthetic.

Writing Style



From the above quote, Makoto Shinkai’s writing style often follows the theme of communication and how there are unavoidable barriers between individuals, examples:



彼女と彼女の猫 [1999]

She and Her Cat

Narrated from perspective of a cat who loves his owner but can’t communicate it due to him being a cat.



君の名は。 [2016]

your name.

The protagonists are unable to communicate with each other easily due to physical distance between one and another and a plot twist put in the story.


Trains are also often incorporated into his works, either as a plot point or as a recurring thing in the background.

Trains are often used to symbolize distance.
